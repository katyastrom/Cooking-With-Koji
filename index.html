What is Koji?

Aspergillus oryzae, also known as koji, is a variety of mold predominantly used in Asia to produce alcoholic beverages and ferment foods. Koji can create things like miso, soy sauce, sake, amazake, and much more. Dubbed Japan's "national mold," koji is essential to Japanese cuisine, along with plenty of other Asian cultures.

How Koji Works

Don't worry! You do not eat this mold on its own. Koji spores, used as a starter to create koji at home, are dusted on top of freshly cooked rice and left to develop for forty-eight hours. After this waiting period, the rice transforms into a fluffy, fragrant, and interesting block of cultivated koji. Pillow-like and smelling of mushrooms and honey, the koji is finally ready for use.

How it's Used

Historically used to make things like soy sauce and sake, the magic of koji is finally helping to develop the flavors of traditionally Western foods. This mold is now beginning to transform things like rotisserie chicken, goat cheese, and hot sauce into umami bombs.

Hard cheeses like parmesan that ordinarily take five years to age properly are made in just two with koji's help. The flavors from a classic chocolate chip cookie can be balanced and rounded out with half a cup of ground koji mixed into sizzling butter.

Koji Marinated Steak

After marinating in koji for 48 hours, your favorite cut of steak is transformed into the most delicious and tender meat you've ever had. In American kitchens, people are just beginning to experiment with the endless possibilities of koji.

Amazake:

Amazake, a popular Japanese beverage, is just one of the many things you can create with koji. Amazake encourages beautiful skin, promotes hair growth, aids in weight loss, relieves fatigue, increases concentration, improves sleep, and so much more. Often called "the world's first red bull" due to its ability to enhance attention, amazake is undoubtedly a super drink. In addition to all of these incredible benefits, amazake is also the easiest way to begin your koji journey. With just three simple ingredients, it is an excellent way to get rid of the intimidation of cooking with koji that people often encounter.
